Semi-Realistic Material Lane

Semi-realistic materials borrow believable lighting and texture cues, but still protect art direction, facial readability, clean values, and stylized control.

Style lane

The bridge lane

Use this lane when the project wants richer surface response than pure stylization, but not full scan realism. Character skin and weathered surfaces often live here.

Principles

ValueKeep broad forms readableDo not let pore detail, dirt, or roughness noise overpower the main form.
SurfaceUse realism selectivelyBorrow roughness, normal, and color variation where they support the design.
CharacterProtect expressionHighlights and micro detail should never fight face planes or emotion.
PipelineConstrain the knobsExpose useful ranges so artists can tune without drifting into plastic or scan realism.

BaselineRealistic PBR SurfaceUse as the grounded reference when semi-realistic work needs calibration.

03

Authoring Rules

1. Decide what stays stylized

Name the surfaces or forms that should stay simplified before adding realistic detail.

2. Review at camera distance

Semi-realistic materials often look good close-up and too noisy in actual gameplay framing.

3. Keep texture detail subordinate

Micro detail should support material identity, not become the main read.
